A tool to examine my own learning

a reference for old ideas
- often find myself needing to reference old code snippets or patterns and maybe other people can benefit from them 

something that motivates me to keep learning

instructional articles to help others learn

The technical landscape for front end devs changes constantly. It seems like every week there's a trendy new 
JS framework that we have to learn and if we don't we're somehow falling behind. That's clearly not the case.
Just because it's new does not necessarily mean it's the best tool for the job. However, I think it's important to 
investigate new popular frameworks so you can make an educated determination if it's the best tool for your use case. This 
means that we as front end folk have to be able to learn new things effectively and quickly. This can often be hard 
to do given there's no syllabus or standardized structured curriculum for Mithril. I see a blog as a way to organize my thoughts 
on a subject 

If you're a font end developer you're a professional student (ironic since lots of us don't have a formal college education).
What I mean is that our field is in a perpetual state of growth and flux. We will constantly have to adapt and learn new things until 
the end of our careers. I look at a blog as a way to analyze and document my learning. It's a tool that forces me to take an honest and 
critical look at my learning style and approach.
